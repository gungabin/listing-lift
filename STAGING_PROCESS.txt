================================================================================
LISTING LIFT — FULL STAGING PROCESS
From user submission → API call → compositing → result
================================================================================
Last updated: 2026-04-10


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
STEP 1 — USER SUBMITS A JOB (Dashboard)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
User uploads a photo and picks:
  - Room type:       living_room | bedroom | dining_room | kitchen | office | bathroom | outdoor | other
  - Decor style:     modern | farmhouse | coastal | traditional | mid_century | scandinavian | transitional
  - Furnishing level: light | medium | full

The photo is stored two ways:
  1. Converted to a blob URL → stored in the job record (for display)
  2. The raw File object → stored in a Map called fileStore, keyed by blob URL (for API calls)

A StagingJob record is written to localStorage and the UI redirects to the job detail page.
stageJob() fires immediately in the background.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
STEP 2 — ENGINE SELECTION (stageJob function)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Priority order:

  1. REPLICATE (if VITE_REPLICATE_API_KEY env var OR ll_replicate_key in localStorage)
     → Uses Flux Fill Pro with auto-generated inpaint mask + post-compositing
     → Best room preservation (two layers of protection)

  2. OPENAI (if VITE_OPENAI_API_KEY env var)
     → Uses gpt-image-1 image edits with verbose preservation prompt + post-compositing
     → Falls back if no Replicate key

  3. MOCK (no key at all)
     → Returns a random Unsplash room photo after 6–10 second delay
     → UI only / no API cost


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
STEP 3A — REPLICATE PATH (runReplicateStaging)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3A-1. IMAGE PREP
  - Retrieve original File from fileStore using the blob URL
  - Convert to PNG using canvas (OpenAI required PNG; Replicate accepts PNG too)
  - Convert PNG to base64 data URI (Replicate accepts data URIs, not blob URLs)

3A-2. MASK GENERATION (generateInpaintMask)
  Auto-generates a black-and-white PNG mask at the same pixel dimensions as the image:

    BLACK (0,0,0)     = PRESERVE — model cannot touch these pixels
    WHITE (255,255,255) = INPAINT  — model generates here

  Zone layout (measured from top of image):
    0%  – 28%   → solid black   (ceiling, upper walls, windows — fully locked)
    28% – 45%   → gradient      (soft transition at wall/floor boundary)
    45% – 100%  → solid white   (furniture zone — model generates here)

  Why 28%/45%:
    Real estate photos typically show ceiling + upper wall in the top 20–30%.
    The gradient at 28–45% prevents a hard seam at the wall/floor boundary.
    Furniture (sofas, chairs, coffee tables) all fit below 45% in wide-angle shots.

  Limitation:
    Tall items like floor lamps, tall bookshelves, or large artwork leaning high
    may be partially cut off if they extend above the 45% line.

3A-3. PROMPT (buildFluxPrompt)
  Short and furniture-focused. The mask handles preservation, so no defensive
  "don't touch the walls" rules are needed.

  Template:
    "Photorealistic interior design photograph. {STYLE}. Furnished {ROOM}. {LEVEL}.
    Furniture has correct perspective and scale for the room, crisp sharp edges,
    soft directional shadows on the floor. Professional real estate photography.
    Natural lighting."

  ROOM values:
    living_room  → "living room with sofa, coffee table, accent chairs, area rug, and decorative accessories"
    bedroom      → "bedroom with bed frame, nightstands, dresser, lamps, and bedding"
    dining_room  → "dining room with dining table, dining chairs, sideboard, and pendant lighting"
    kitchen      → "kitchen with bar stools at island or counter, small decorative accessories on countertops"
    office       → "home office with desk, office chair, bookshelf, desk lamp, and accessories"
    bathroom     → "bathroom with towels, bath mat, vanity accessories, and small decor"
    outdoor      → "outdoor patio with outdoor furniture, planters, and outdoor accessories"
    other        → "room with appropriate furniture and decor"

  STYLE values:
    modern        → "sleek modern style with clean lines, neutral tones, minimal clutter, high-contrast accents, polished surfaces"
    farmhouse     → "warm farmhouse style with shiplap textures, natural wood tones, linen fabrics, vintage accents, cozy rustic warmth"
    coastal       → "coastal style with light blues, whites, natural textures, woven accents, breezy and relaxed atmosphere"
    traditional   → "classic traditional style with rich wood tones, symmetrical arrangements, formal elegance, warm deep colors"
    mid_century   → "mid-century modern style with tapered legs, warm wood tones, earthy palette, retro geometric patterns"
    scandinavian  → "Scandinavian style with white walls, light wood, minimal clean design, cozy hygge textures"
    transitional  → "transitional style blending traditional and modern, neutral palette, sophisticated and timeless"

  LEVEL values:
    light  → "Add only a few key accent pieces — one or two small furniture items, minimal accessories. Keep it sparse and open."
    medium → "Add the main furniture pieces for this room type and a moderate amount of accessories. Balanced and livable."
    full   → "Fully stage the room with complete furniture arrangement, layered textiles, art, plants, and rich accessories. Lush and complete."

  Example assembled prompt (Living Room / Transitional / Medium):
    "Photorealistic interior design photograph. transitional style blending
    traditional and modern, neutral palette, sophisticated and timeless.
    Furnished living room with sofa, coffee table, accent chairs, area rug,
    and decorative accessories. Add the main furniture pieces for this room
    type and a moderate amount of accessories. Balanced and livable. Furniture
    has correct perspective and scale for the room, crisp sharp edges, soft
    directional shadows on the floor. Professional real estate photography.
    Natural lighting."

3A-4. API CALL
  Endpoint:  POST /replicate-api/v1/models/black-forest-labs/flux-fill-pro/predictions
             (proxied through Vite dev server → https://api.replicate.com)
  Model:     black-forest-labs/flux-fill-pro
  Cost:      $0.05 per image

  Parameters sent:
    image         → base64 PNG data URI of the original room photo
    mask          → base64 PNG data URI of the auto-generated mask
    prompt        → assembled text prompt (see 3A-3)
    steps         → 50  (max quality; range 15–50)
    guidance      → 60  (high prompt adherence; range 1.5–100)
    output_format → "png"
    safety_tolerance → 6  (most permissive; range 1–6)

  Headers:
    Authorization: Bearer {REPLICATE_API_KEY}
    Prefer: wait=60  (sync response if completes within 60 seconds)

  Response:
    If status === "succeeded" → output is a URL to the generated PNG
    Otherwise → poll every 3 seconds at /replicate-api/v1/predictions/{id}
    Timeout after 60 poll attempts (~3 minutes)

3A-5. OUTPUT
  The Replicate output URL is a temporary CDN link. We:
    1. Fetch it as a blob
    2. Create a local blob URL for display
    3. Pass it to compositeStructure() (see Step 4)

STEP 3B — OPENAI PATH (runAiStaging)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3B-1. IMAGE PREP
  - Retrieve original File from fileStore
  - Convert to PNG (required by OpenAI)

3B-2. PROMPT (buildStagingPrompt)
  Very verbose — must describe preservation rules because gpt-image-1 regenerates
  the entire scene and has no mask support via the edits endpoint.

  Full prompt text:

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  "This is a real estate photograph of an empty room. Your ONLY task is to
  place freestanding furniture and accessories into the empty space. You are
  adding objects to a photograph — nothing about the photograph itself changes.
  Every pixel not covered by furniture you add must be 100% identical to the
  original image.

  ═══════════════════════════════════════
  PRESERVATION RULES — READ THESE FIRST
  These are absolute. Violating any one is a complete failure.
  ═══════════════════════════════════════

  FLOOR — MOST CRITICAL:
  The floor is a fixed, permanent feature of this property. It must appear
  exactly as it does in the original photograph in every area not directly
  covered by furniture you place.
  - DO NOT change the floor color, stain, tone, or finish in any way
  - DO NOT lighten, darken, warm, cool, or recolor the flooring
  - DO NOT change the wood species, plank width, grain pattern, or texture
  - DO NOT add sheen, gloss, or polish that does not exist in the original
  - DO NOT blur, sharpen, or alter the floor surface in any way
  - Floor visible around furniture legs, between pieces, and at room edges
    must be pixel-perfect identical to the original
  - If the original floor looks raw, unfinished, or light in tone — it must
    remain exactly that way

  WINDOWS & EXTERIOR VIEW:
  - Every window must look exactly as it does in the original — same frame,
    same glass, same exterior view
  - If the exterior view is overexposed, blown out, bright white, or partially
    visible — it must remain that way. DO NOT add trees, sky, foliage,
    buildings, or any scenery not clearly present in the original
  - DO NOT add curtains, blinds, drapes, shutters, or any window treatments
    of any kind
  - DO NOT alter window frames, glass reflections, or mullions

  WALLS:
  - Wall color, paint, texture, paneling, wainscoting, board-and-batten,
    shiplap, millwork, wallpaper — must be pixel-perfect identical to the
    original
  - DO NOT alter, enhance, brighten, or "improve" any wall surface

  CEILING:
  - Ceiling color, texture, coffered details, tray ceiling profiles, beams,
    crown molding — must be pixel-perfect identical to the original
  - DO NOT alter, lighten, or enhance the ceiling in any way

  FIXED FIXTURES — every one of these must remain exactly as photographed:
  - Recessed lights, can lights, ceiling fans, chandeliers, pendants, sconces
  - HVAC vents, air returns, grilles, diffusers — exact appearance preserved
  - Electrical outlets, switch plates, wall plates, coax plates, ethernet plates
  - Smoke detectors, carbon monoxide detectors, sprinkler heads
  - Thermostats, keypads, wall-mounted devices
  - Baseboards, door frames, doors, built-in shelving, fireplaces,
    built-in cabinetry
  - If furniture would overlap a fixture, you may place furniture in front —
    but never alter the fixture itself

  LIGHTING & ATMOSPHERE:
  - The room's existing natural and artificial lighting must remain unchanged
  - DO NOT adjust brightness, contrast, exposure, saturation, or color
    temperature of the room
  - DO NOT add ambient light, fill light, or HDR enhancement to the space
  - Only add cast shadows directly beneath furniture you place, matched to
    the existing light direction

  ═══════════════════════════════════════
  WHAT YOU ARE ALLOWED TO ADD
  ═══════════════════════════════════════
  - Freestanding furniture placed on visible floor areas (sofas, chairs,
    tables, beds, bookshelves)
  - A single area rug placed under/beneath furniture groupings
  - Small accessories placed on top of furniture you added (lamps, plants,
    books, vases, bowls, art leaned against walls)
  - All additions must match the room's existing perspective, lighting,
    and scale exactly
  - The final image must be photorealistic — indistinguishable from a
    real photograph

  ═══════════════════════════════════════
  STAGING REQUIREMENTS
  ═══════════════════════════════════════
  Room: {ROOM_DESCRIPTOR}
  Style: {STYLE_DESCRIPTOR}
  Density: {LEVEL_DESCRIPTOR}

  FINAL CHECK: Before finishing, verify — do the floors, walls, ceiling,
  windows, and all fixtures look exactly as they did in the original photo?
  If anything structural has changed, that is a failure. Undo it."
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3B-3. API CALL
  Endpoint:  POST https://api.openai.com/v1/images/edits
  Model:     gpt-image-1
  Cost:      ~$0.04–0.08 per image (high quality)

  Parameters sent (multipart/form-data):
    model   → "gpt-image-1"
    prompt  → assembled text prompt (see 3B-2)
    image   → PNG file of original room photo
    quality → "high"
    size    → "1536x1024"

  Response:
    data[0].b64_json → base64-encoded PNG of the staged image

3B-4. OUTPUT
  Decode base64 → blob → local blob URL → pass to compositeStructure()


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
STEP 4 — FLOOR COMPOSITING (compositeStructure)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Applied AFTER both Replicate and OpenAI outputs. Restores the original floor.

APPROACH: Color-distance per pixel in the floor zone.

  Zone layout:
    0%  – 45% from top → skipped entirely (walls, ceiling — AI got these right via mask)
    45% – 55% from top → transition zone (gradual blend)
    55% – 100% from top → full floor zone

  For each pixel in the floor zone:
    1. Calculate Euclidean color distance between original pixel and AI pixel:
       dist = sqrt((aiR-origR)² + (aiG-origG)² + (aiB-origB)²)

    2. If dist < FLOOR_THRESHOLD (currently 120):
       → AI just recolored/replaced the floor → RESTORE original pixel
       (Weighted by zone strength: gradual in transition zone, full restore in floor zone)

    3. If dist ≥ FLOOR_THRESHOLD:
       → Something physically different is here (rug, furniture leg, shadow) → KEEP AI pixel

  FLOOR_THRESHOLD = 120
    - Was 55 (tuned for OpenAI's subtle recoloring)
    - Raised to 120 for Flux Fill which does full floor replacement
    - Risk: if rug is close in tone to original floor (both light/beige),
      threshold may not catch all rug pixels cleanly → patchy edges
    - Too high → rug starts getting erased
    - Too low → floor still changes

KNOWN ISSUE:
  When the AI-generated rug is close in color to the original light wood floor
  (both beige/light tones), the color distance is small and threshold comparison
  incorrectly restores the original floor in those pixels, causing patchy/torn
  edges at the rug boundary. This is the core unresolved compositing problem.

STEP 5 — JOB COMPLETION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  - Job status updated to "completed" in localStorage
  - staged_image_url set to the composited blob URL
  - generations_used incremented in subscription record
  - Job detail page (polling every 3s) detects "completed" and shows the result
  - User sees AI Staged / Original toggle


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OPEN PROBLEMS (as of 2026-04-10)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. FLOOR COMPOSITING ARTIFACTS
   Light rugs on light floors → beige-on-beige → small color distance →
   compositing incorrectly restores original floor pixels inside the rug →
   patchy/torn rug edges. Need a better segmentation approach.

2. MASK BOUNDARY
   The 28%/45% mask boundary is hardcoded. A room with an unusually low
   ceiling or extreme wide-angle lens could have the ceiling bleed below 28%.
   Future: generate mask from depth estimation rather than fixed percentage.

3. FLOOR STILL CHANGES SLIGHTLY IN TRANSITION ZONE
   The 45–55% gradient means some floor pixels near the baseboard are only
   partially restored. Usually not visible but can show on short rooms.

4. BLOB URLS LOST ON REFRESH
   Blob URLs (for uploaded photos and staged results) are session-only.
   On refresh, jobs show as completed but images are gone. This is expected
   for local dev — real backend will use persistent storage URLs.

5. CORS IN PRODUCTION
   The Vite proxy only works in local dev. Vercel deployment needs a
   serverless function at /api/replicate that forwards calls server-side.
